Mercury

Closest to the Sun, Mercury is a small, rocky planet with extreme temperatures ranging from scorching to freezing. It has a thin atmosphere and cratered surface, resembling the Moon.

Venus

Known for its thick, toxic atmosphere, Venus is similar in size to Earth but experiences extreme heat due to a runaway greenhouse effect. Its surface is dominated by volcanoes and rocky plains, with no visible water.

Earth

The only known planet to support life, Earth has a diverse environment with oceans, continents, and an atmosphere conducive to life as we know it. It's home to a vast array of ecosystems and millions of species.

Mars

Often called the "Red Planet" due to its iron-rich soil, Mars features polar ice caps, canyons, and extinct volcanoes. It has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with ongoing exploration for signs of past or present life.

05

Jupiter

The largest planet in our solar system, Jupiter is a gas giant with a turbulent atmosphere of colorful bands and the iconic Great Red Spot, a massive storm. It has numerous moons, including the largest, Ganymede, and a powerful magnetic field.

06

Saturn

Known for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock particles, Saturn is another gas giant. It has a complex atmosphere and a diverse array of moons, including the large moon Titan, which has a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane and ethane.

07

Uranus

A distant ice giant with a tilted axis, Uranus rotates on its side. It has a blue-green hue due to methane in its atmosphere and a system of rings and moons, though less prominent than Saturn's.

08

Neptune

The outermost planet in our solar system, Neptune is an ice giant with a vivid blue coloration. It has a dynamic atmosphere with high-speed winds and a moon named Triton, which orbits it in a retrograde direction. Neptune's composition includes icy materials and a rocky core.
